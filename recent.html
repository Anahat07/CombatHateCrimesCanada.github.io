What Are the Current Findings on Hate Crimes in Canada?

According to data from Statistics Canada, there has been a noticeable increase in reported hate crimes over the past decade. This rise is attributed to several factors, including better reporting mechanisms, increased public awareness, and possibly a genuine increase in incidents fueled by global and domestic socio-political tensions. Read more below.

Specific Statistics

Here are some recent statistics found by Statistics Canada:

  • The number of police-reported hate crimes rose by 7% in 2022, continuing an upward trend with an 83% increase from 2019 to 2022 (Department of Justice, 2024).
  • Hate crimes motivated by race or ethnicity increased by 12% in 2022, marking the fourth consecutive year of increase (Department of Justice, 2024).
  • Hate crimes motivated by religion decreased by 15% overall in 2022, after peaking in 2021, with notable changes (Department of Justice, 2024).
  • Hate crimes motivated by sexual orientation increased by 12% in 2022 (Department of Justice, 2024).
  • The majority of police-reported hate crimes remained non-violent in 2022. Non-violent hate crimes rose by 3%, while violent hate crimes increased by 12% (Department of Justice, 2024).
