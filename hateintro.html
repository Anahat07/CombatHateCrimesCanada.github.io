What Are Hate Crimes in Canada?

Hate crimes in Canada are defined as criminal violations motivated by hate based on various factors, including race, national or ethnic origin, language, colour, religion, sex, age, mental or physical disability, sexual orientation, or gender identity or expression (Ndegwa & McDonald, 2023). They are a pressing and prevalent issue in Canada, reflecting deep-seated prejudices and societal divisions. These crimes impact not only the direct victims but also the larger communities they represent, instilling fear and division.
